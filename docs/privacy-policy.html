PurpleBear

Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 22, 2026

PurpleBear is an Android network utility app. This Privacy Policy explains what information the app accesses, how that information is used, and what choices users have.

1. Information the app can access

  • Camera access: The app may request camera permission only when you use the QR code scanning feature to import links or configuration data.
  • Network information: The app uses network access to fetch subscription links, test node latency, update rule sources, and establish VPN or proxy connections.
  • Local configuration data: The app stores node settings, subscriptions, local rules, selected options, and related app preferences on your device so the app can function.
  • VPN traffic: When you enable the VPN feature, your device traffic may be routed through servers or subscription services that you choose or import into the app.

2. How information is used

  • To scan QR codes that you choose to scan.
  • To import and manage subscription links and node configurations.
  • To establish, maintain, diagnose, and measure VPN or proxy connections.
  • To apply app settings such as per-app proxy, routing rules, and media routing behavior.

3. Camera permission

The CAMERA permission is used only for QR code scanning initiated by the user. PurpleBear does not use the camera for background capture, recording, advertising, or analytics. QR scan results are processed locally in the app to import configuration content.

4. Data collection and sharing

PurpleBear is designed as a client-side utility app. The app itself does not sell your personal data. The app does not use the camera to upload photos or video content to the developer.

However, if you import third-party subscription links, connect to third-party proxy or VPN servers, or use third-party network resources, those third parties may process your traffic or configuration data according to their own policies. You are responsible for reviewing the privacy and security practices of the services and servers you choose to use.

5. Local storage

Configuration data such as nodes, subscriptions, selected routes, and preferences may be stored locally on your device so that the app can continue working between sessions.

6. Security

Reasonable efforts are made to keep the app functioning as intended, but no method of transmission or storage is completely secure. Users should avoid importing untrusted configuration sources.

7. Children

PurpleBear is not directed to children.

8. Changes to this policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. The latest version published at this page will apply.

9. Contact

If you have privacy questions about PurpleBear, please use the developer contact information provided on the Google Play listing for the app.
